About

Alison is a driven third-year computer science student at Northeastern University, specializing in Artificial Intelligence with a deep interest in inclusive and user-centered design. She is passionate about leveraging technology to create innovative solutions that address real-world challenges, particularly those that empower underrepresented groups and communities. Although Alison is still building her expertise in coding, machine learning, and databases, she is eager to expand her knowledge and apply her skills in meaningful ways.

Throughout her academic journey, Alison has focused on developing a strong problem-solving mindset, which she applies to both individual projects and team collaborations. She believes in the power of technology to not only innovate but also to foster inclusivity, making it accessible to all users, regardless of their backgrounds or abilities. As part of her commitment to this mission, Alison has worked on several projects aimed at improving accessibility in technology and education.

In addition to her academic work, Alison is actively involved in creating opportunities for others to explore STEM fields. She volunteers as part of a club that offers tech workshops for high school students, helping to increase diversity and representation in these fields. Alison’s dedication to both personal growth and community outreach reflects her long-term goal of using her skills to make a tangible, positive impact on the world.
