About

I am a driven third-year computer science student at Northeastern University, specializing in Artificial Intelligence with a deep interest in inclusive and user-centered design. I am passionate about leveraging technology to create innovative solutions that address real-world challenges, particularly those that empower underrepresented groups and communities. Although I am still building her expertise in coding, machine learning, and databases, I am eager to expand my knowledge and apply my skills in meaningful ways.

Throughout my academic journey, I have focused on developing a strong problem-solving mindset, which I have applied to both individual projects and team initiatives. I believe in the power of technology to not only innovate but also to foster inclusivity, making it accessible to all users, regardless of their backgrounds or abilities. I have taken classes where I studied, and I am continuing to learn outside of school, how to make technology more inclusive.

In addition to my academic work, I am actively involved in creating opportunities for others to explore STEM fields. I am a member of FirstByte, a club that offers tech workshops for high school students, helping to increase diversity and representation in these fields. My dedication to both personal growth and community outreach reflects my long-term goal of using my skills to make a tangible, positive impact on the world.

Medicaid Drug Spending Analysis

Team Member

January 2025 - April 2025

An analysis of Medicaid drug spending from 2018 to 2022 using Python. I read in the dataset using the CMS API. In my role, I grouped and filtered the dataset to identify the top 5 pharmaceutical companies by Medicaid spending received in 2022. To forecast future trends, I applied a simple linear regression model to predict Medicaid spending from 2025 to 2026. I visualized these insights using Seaborn, Plotly, and Matplotlib to clearly communicate patterns and projections.
